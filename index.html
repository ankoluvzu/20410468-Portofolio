My journey

Hello, my name is Madalina, and I am originally from Romania, growing up I was always passionate about computers, I remember when I first got my brand-new desktop the next day I opened and disassembled everything just to see how it works, after the next week I took my Windows XP down and took me 7 tries to learn how to install it...you can guess how happy my parents were...

Growing up I developed plenty of passions and skills around the wonderful world of web leading to my actual passion, web design. When I discovered Shopify and building stores online, I was instantly hooked up into building stores and later developed into this excitement of building anything in WordPress and Shopify mainly because I was limited to page builders and learning how to code seemed impossible for me to learn

This is until I took my heart into my hands and decided to go to university, so I can learn more about web development, unfortunately in Romania at that time there were no universities that were teaching computing, web development especially, so I took the next step, I applied to university in UK leaving faith to decide if this was the next step to take.

As you can guess I got into university in Northampton, so I took my belongings and left everything I had back home, so I can come here and study my passion.

A picture of my cousin and my first computer

My year so far...

I started university last year with the Foundation year, initially I was disappointed by the idea to take an extra year to finish, but during the course of the last year I was impressed on the "Learning how to learn" courses, they taught me valuable information about what you need to do correctly to finish university.

At the beginning of this year I was scared by the amount of skills I have to learn in a relatively so little time, but this showed me that if you try and stay consistent with learning... Well it's not so hard to learn a new skill even though it will take a lot of time to become good at it, this is only my first year.

The courses were a little hard for me as English is not my native language and some concepts were hard to understand especially the technical words, but in time I got used to it and I can say that the university life taught me more English that I can imagine.
