My site report

My experience building this website

I started the website design by drawing a mock design on paper and following the guidelines, although it seemed like I already learned everything I needed from classes I pretty fast hit a wall when it came to applying this knowledge to the website, I made a few mistakes in the beginning, one was using the flex box to design almost half of the website, after carefully reading the brief again I noticed my mistake and went back to correct it, the second mistake was when I made the blog page I read in between the lines of the brief so when the blog page was ready I realised I needed a different design and approach.

I corrected this fast as I refurbished the already made code to be my portfolio page as the code fitted perfectly there, for the blog page I had fun creating the design to look like a standard WordPress blog page, with "fake" links to imitate the real use of a link in some parts of the text but this was not possible on my machine so I just left the design as it is. On the portfolio page I made a clean presentation of some of my active projects with links integrated into them so you can visit and interact with the websites, unfortunately a lot of my past projects were closed by their owners so I couldn't add a link but I still had some screenshots so I refurbished them into a website mock design.

I created the contact page where I added a banner reminding the person visiting who I am and what I am doing, along with that I added my social media links along with images and mock icons for personal contact (WhatsApp, email, and phone), alongside that sits a contact form banner with the contact form and an image reassuring people that a real person is behind the website. Finally, I added the site report were I am writing this text, the page will display my original website mock design, my page validations that are to my astonishment on a healthy percentage at first and only a few changes were needed to get a pass validation. My view on this project is that although I worked for a few years with page builders to build websites being afraid to learn how to code because it seemed at that time an impossible thing for me to perceive, moving from Romania to follow my passion and starting the university I can see a huge amount of experience and skills I have built in only 1 year, I cannot wait to see the future projects that I will have as I can sincerely say I enjoy studying here and practicing everything not only theories make me really love gaining more skills.

My design fonts and colors

I have choosen a color that resonates with me for this portofolio, in this case it was purple with different variations of it to create the gradients while keeping the design simple. I had different fonts to choose from, but the one i have left in the end was "'Segoe UI', Tahoma, Geneva, Verdana, sans-serif;" as it looks good and it is easy to read by the visual impaired persons.
